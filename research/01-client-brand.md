## Visual Analysis

### Logo
- Deep black square background.
- "GULF COAST / CLINICAL CONSULTING" wordmark in a refined uppercase serif with generous tracking.
- Teal (#0E9F8F approx.) interlocking script "GC" monogram — this teal is the secondary color the client asked for, and it's already baked into the mark.
- The mark reads: authoritative, quiet, medical-legal (not clinic-cheerful, not law-firm-stuffy).

### Founder Portrait
- Warm, natural light. Confident smile. Navy blazer + pearl necklace + white blouse. Framed diplomas visible in the background — subconscious credentialing signal.
- Composition is squareish and centered — ideal for a hero portrait with left-aligned copy on desktop and stacked mobile.
- The framed credentials in the background are a gift: they visually reinforce the "credentialed expert" positioning without adding a single word.

## Palette Decision

The client stated: **Black, White, Red, Teal.** The logo confirms black + teal. Red is the wildcard — legal nurse consulting is a trust-heavy field, so red must be used surgically. Recommendation:

| Role | Hex | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Ink (dominant) | `#0A0A0A` | Body text, dark section backgrounds, hero background |
| Paper | `#FFFFFF` | Light section backgrounds, cards |
| Teal (primary accent) | `#0E9F8F` | Section headers, iconography, hover states, monogram callouts |
| Teal (deep) | `#0A5B54` | Buttons on light backgrounds, deep-tone gradients |
| Red (accent, restrained) | `#C8102E` | Single primary CTA color, credential badges, one-line eyebrow accents only |
| Bone (warm neutral) | `#F5F2EC` | Alternative light section for visual rhythm |
| Muted ink | `#4A4A4A` | Secondary body text, captions |

Red is **not** used as a full section background or as body text. It's a spotlight color: the "Book a Consultation" CTA, an underline on the hero keyword, and the eyebrow flourish on the credentials strip.

## Typography Decision

- **Headings:** `Playfair Display` (weight 600–700). Serif that echoes the logo's typographic personality. Confident but not stuffy.
- **Eyebrows / small caps:** `Inter` uppercase, tracked at `.16em`, 12–13px. Modern counterpoint.
- **Body:** `Inter` (weights 400 + 500 + 600). High readability, medico-legal appropriate.
- **Numerals for stats:** `Playfair Display` italic — feels editorial, not dashboard-y.

---

## Tone Guardrails

Do:
- Speak plainly. Attorneys are busy.
- Lead with credentials once, then let the work speak.
- Use precise verbs: analyze, review, testify, distill, translate, corroborate.
- Reference specific case types (birth injury, nursing home neglect, medical malpractice, product liability, personal injury).

Avoid:
- Hospital-marketing warmth ("caring team," "your health journey").
- Law-firm bombast ("aggressive advocacy," "we fight for you").
- Any suggestion Dr. Littles is providing legal advice — she is a clinical expert supporting counsel, not a lawyer.
