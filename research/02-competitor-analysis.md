## Patterns of the Top 10%

Cross-referencing the highest-scoring competitors reveals five patterns Dr. Littles' site must adopt — and one opportunity to leapfrog the whole niche.

### 1. Named founder = trust multiplier
Alexan (Nancy Breedlove) and Legally Yours (Laura Brower) both feature founders on the homepage. Attorneys hire *people*, not firms. **Dr. Littles' portrait belongs in the hero.**

### 2. Testimonials from named attorneys, above the fold or one scroll down
OnPoint, Med League, Alexan, Legally Yours all place attorney testimonials prominently. Anonymous quotes are worthless in this niche — a named partner from a named firm is the currency.

### 3. CTA verb matters
"Submit a Case" (OnPoint), "Request an Expert" (Med League), "Ring for the Nurse" (Alexan) all beat "Contact us" or "Learn more" (MRN). Dr. Littles' primary CTA should be action-verb, case-specific.

### 4. Service list must be specific, not generic
Every top site enumerates: medical record review, chronologies, merit screening, expert witness location, life care planning, IME coordination, deposition prep. Attorneys need to see the exact deliverables named.

### 5. Case-type verticals build authority
Alexan and OnPoint list case categories (mass tort, malpractice, product liability, PI). Attorneys who work in these lanes want to see themselves reflected.

### THE OPPORTUNITY: The whole niche is visually asleep
Every single competitor above is blue-and-white and design-scores 5-7 out of 10. There is no scroll motion, no serif elegance, no cinematic feel. **Dr. Littles' brand — deep black, teal, red-accented, GSAP-animated, Playfair Display serif — will look like an Apple keynote sitting next to a 2014 legal-vendor directory.** This is a positioning gift.

## SEO Landscape

Attorneys searching for LNC services use these query patterns:

- "legal nurse consultant [state / metro]" (e.g., "legal nurse consultant Houston", "legal nurse consultant Gulf Coast")
- "medical record review for attorneys"
- "medical malpractice case merit review"
- "nursing home neglect expert nurse"
- "birth injury legal nurse consultant"
- "life care planner [state]"

**Gaps in the niche:**
- Few sites optimize for **regional intent** (Gulf Coast, Texas, Louisiana, Mississippi, Alabama, Florida panhandle). Dr. Littles' geographic brand name is an underused SEO asset.
- Few sites write case-type-specific service pages. A dedicated `/services/nursing-home-neglect` page would rank easily.
- Almost none of the sites publish a founder credential deep-dive. Dr. Littles' DNP + MSN + RN-BC stack is title-tag gold.

**Recommended primary keywords for launch:**
1. `legal nurse consultant Gulf Coast`
2. `medical record review attorneys Texas` (or client's specific market)
3. `nursing malpractice expert witness`
4. `medical malpractice merit review`
5. `legal nurse consultant DNP`
