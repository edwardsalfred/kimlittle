Executive Summary

Not one competitor animates on scroll. Not one uses a serif hero. This is a gift: Gulf Coast Clinical Consulting can enter the market with a distinctive black, teal, and red identity, a founder-face hero, and cinematic motion, and read as the most premium option on day one.

SEO Landscape

Attorneys search the LNC niche with a small vocabulary. The queries below repeat across search behavior data:

  • "legal nurse consultant [state / metro]"
  • "medical record review for attorneys"
  • "medical malpractice case merit review"
  • "nursing home neglect expert nurse"
  • "birth injury legal nurse consultant"
  • "life care planner [state]"
Gap: regional intent Gap: case-type pages Gap: credentialed founder page

Gulf Coast Clinical Consulting has a naming advantage. Regional keywords like "Gulf Coast legal nurse consultant" have low competition and high commercial intent. Owning a founder-credential page (DNP + MSN + RN-BC) will differentiate against a niche where most owners are BSN-only.

05

Patterns of the Top 10%

1

Named founder equals trust multiplier

Attorneys hire people, not firms. Alexan and Legally Yours both feature founders on the homepage and out-convert peers who hide behind stock imagery.

2

Named-attorney testimonials, prominently placed

Anonymous quotes are worthless in this niche. A named partner from a named firm is the currency. Every high-scoring site places these within the first two scroll depths.

3

Action-verb CTAs, not "Contact us"

"Submit a Case," "Request an Expert," "Ring for the Nurse" all beat "Learn More." The MRN weakness is a direct case study.

4

Specific deliverables, not generic categories

Attorneys need to see the exact artifact they will receive: chronology, merit letter, IME coordination, life care plan, deposition prep. Vague service labels lose to specific ones.

5

Case-type verticals build authority

Mass tort, malpractice, product liability, PI, nursing home neglect. Attorneys who work in these lanes want to see themselves reflected in the copy.

06

Recommended Direction

Palette

Ink black + teal (from the logo monogram) + a red spotlight for the primary CTA only. White for calm, warm bone (#F5F2EC) for section rhythm. Every competitor is blue and white; the delta is instant.

Typography

Playfair Display for headlines, Inter for body. Editorial serif signals authority. No competitor in the niche uses a serif.

Hero art

Dr. Littles' portrait, right-aligned on desktop, framed diplomas visible. Left-aligned copy leads with credentials and a case-review CTA.

Motion

GSAP + ScrollTrigger throughout. Section reveals, parallax on the portrait, gentle counter-ups on stats, restrained micro-interactions on cards. No competitor animates. Instant modernity signal.

Site architecture

Home, About Dr. Littles (credentials deep-dive), Services (with sub-headers for case-type verticals), Case Studies / For Attorneys, Contact / Request a Review. Regional SEO baked into titles.

Primary CTA

"Request a Case Review." Verb-forward, industry-standard, matches attorney search intent. Red spotlight color; the only red on the page.
